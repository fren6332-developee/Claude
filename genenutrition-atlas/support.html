Frequently asked questions

Is GeneNutrition Atlas medical advice?

No. It is an educational reference only — not medical advice, a diagnosis, a treatment, or a medical device. Always talk to your clinician before changing any medication, supplement, or diet.

Where do the facts come from?

Content is compiled and cross-referenced against the APA, NIMH, ISNPR, the Food & Mood Centre, the American Society for Nutrition, the Global Brain Health Institute, and CPIC/PharmGKB. The full source list and methodology are in the app's “Safety & Sources” screen.

Does the app collect my data?

No. It runs entirely on your device, with no account, no tracking, and no data collection. See our Privacy Policy.

Has the content been reviewed by a clinician?

The content is compiled from cited sources and is currently pending independent clinical review. The app shows this status transparently.

How do I use the audio narration?

Open any gene and tap “Listen.” Each gene has a fixed, studio-recorded narration that works offline.

If you are in crisis

If you are in the U.S. and thinking about suicide or self-harm, call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or call 911 in an emergency. International helplines: findahelpline.com.
